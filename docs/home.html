Alphabetical Order in the European Middle Ages Day

I declare August 7 to be Alphabetical Order in the European Middle Ages Day. It may be celebrated with public readings of Richard and Mary Rouse's article "Statim invenire", the burning (ok ok, the mocking) of works by early modernists claiming that alphabetical organization came in with the printing press, or just a moment of personal reflection while arranging the books on the coffee table alphabetically. (The date is half way between the Rouses' birthdays: Mary's on July 31 and Richard's on August 14.) (Cardinal Hugh of Saint-Cher, leader of the Dominican team that made the alphabetical concordance of scripture in Paris in the 1220s, could be the day's Patron Saint in Waiting, as he's in Wikipedia's list of probable Dominican candidates for sainthood.)
